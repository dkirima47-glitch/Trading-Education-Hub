📖 Trading Glossary

Common trading terms explained in plain English.

Ask Price

The lowest price a seller is willing to accept for a security. Also called the offer price.

Bid Price

The highest price a buyer is willing to pay for a security.

Spread

The difference between the bid and ask price. Tighter spreads mean lower trading costs.

Leverage

Using borrowed capital to increase potential returns. Amplifies both gains AND losses.

Margin

The amount of money required to open a leveraged position. Usually expressed as a percentage.

Stop Loss

An order to automatically sell a security when it reaches a certain price, limiting potential losses.

Take Profit

An order to automatically sell a security when it reaches a target profit level.

Bull Market

A market condition where prices are rising or expected to rise. Optimistic sentiment prevails.

Bear Market

A market condition where prices are falling or expected to fall. Pessimistic sentiment prevails.

Volume

The number of shares or contracts traded in a security during a given period. High volume = high liquidity.

Volatility

A statistical measure of price fluctuations. High volatility = larger price swings and higher risk.

Liquidity

How easily an asset can be bought or sold without affecting its price. Highly liquid = tight spreads.
