Trading Psychology

Definition

Trading psychology refers to the emotional and mental factors that influence trading decisions. It examines how thoughts, emotions, and behavioral biases affect performance in financial markets.

  • Emotional Control: Learn to manage fear and greed, which can lead to impulsive decisions.
  • Discipline: Stick to your trading plan and avoid deviating based on short-term market movements.
  • Patience: Wait for the right opportunities rather than forcing trades.
  • Self-awareness: Recognize your strengths and weaknesses as a trader.
